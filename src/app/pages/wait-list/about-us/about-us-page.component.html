Introduction

In the complex landscape of rare diseases, understanding patient experiences and outcomes is essential.

Central to this understanding is Patient Experience Data (PED), which encompasses the insights, outcomes, and treatment preferences shared by patients. This data is crucial for improving healthcare services, particularly in developing treatments for rare diseases (known as orphan medicines).

Despite its importance, European healthcare organizations face challenges in effectively collecting, processing, and utilizing PED. This includes a lack of standardized methodologies, insufficient data collection culture within patient organizations, and the need for effective strategies to initiate data capture.

What does Patient Experience Data (PED) involve?

PED refers to the information and insights derived directly from patients and their caregivers about their experiences with a disease or condition and its treatment. It encompasses a wide range of data types, including:

  • Symptoms experienced by patients and how these symptoms affect their day-to-day lives.
  • Quality of life, including the emotional, social, and physical impact of the disease
  • Treatment preferences and outcomes, reflecting what is important to patients in terms of care, treatment options, and the implications of these treatments on their lives.
  • Disease progression and management, detailing how patients navigate their conditions over time, including the effectiveness and side effects of treatments.

PED is all-encompassing and seeks to gather holistic insights into the patient's journey, highlighting what matters most to those affected by rare diseases, where individual experiences can vary widely and may not be well-understood.

What is this project about?

Collaborare emerges from the imperative need to improve patient care by strategically capturing and utilizing PED. The project addresses the challenges faced in gathering this data for orphan medicines.

By leveraging PED, Collaborare aligns with the goals of the European Medicines Agency (EMA) to incorporate patient insights into the development and evaluation of medicines, ensuring that treatments truly meet patient needs.

What is Collaborare?

Collaborare offers an innovative solution to these challenges by developing a patient-validated methodology that utilizes artificial intelligence (AI) to streamline the initial collection of PED.

By identifying and validating PED that contribute significantly to patient care, Collaborare seeks to improve healthcare decision-making and support regulatory processes for rare diseases’ treatments.

How does it work?

The process is straightforward and user-friendly:

  • Generative AI is employed to analyze existing information for a specific condition, generating a preliminary list of key issues and needs.
  • Patient organizations nominate a representative to register to the Collaborare website.
  • This representative by collecting consensus in the organisation reviews, prioritizes, and completes the list of items.
  • The validated list is structured into an accessible format and published as open data, driving towards a deeper understanding of patient needs and innovative solutions.
  • After validation, the tool indicates the date and the organisation which has validated the list.

For detailed step-by-step guidance, please review the How-to Guide.
